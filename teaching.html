Teaching & Mentorship

Training the next generation in optics, computation, and experimental rigor.

Instruction emphasizes reproducibility, rigorous physical modeling, and hands-on systems development with a focus on student independence and scientific communication.

Course Portfolio

  • ESE 524 — Detection and Estimation Theory. Spring 2023 syllabus with lecture and recitation structure for probability, estimation, and detection in engineering systems.
  • ESE 417 — Advanced signal processing and communications foundations. See the WashU ESE course offerings page for undergraduate catalog and course planning.
  • ESE 438 — Applied Optics. Referenced in WashU’s undergraduate ESE course catalog and department course offerings as the core optics elective for imaging and photonics systems.
  • ESE 553 — Nonlinear Dynamic Systems. Graduate Bulletin listing for theory-driven analysis of nonlinear and adaptive engineering systems.
  • Fudan Optical Fiber Communication — 光纤通信原理与技术. See Fudan University School of Information Science and Technology course page for undergraduate optical fiber communication offerings.

Teaching Approach

Courses are taught with a blended focus on physical intuition, mathematical rigor, and practical laboratory implementation. Students learn how optical systems behave under noise, nonlinearities, and real-world constraints.
