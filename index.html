ARISE

A resonant-field-based physical information reconstruction framework.

VISION

Information acquisition is the foundation of how both humans and intelligent systems understand and act upon the world. As AI, precision medicine, and advanced manufacturing converge, sensing is shifting from measuring a single physical quantity to perceiving the full state of a complex system.

We aim to fundamentally change this paradigm.

Instead of improving individual sensors, we develop a framework for reconstructing latent physical information from resonant field interactions.

ARISE — A Research Framework

ARISE is an acoustic–resonator–informed sensing and estimation framework that integrates optical whispering-gallery resonators, photoacoustic transduction, and physics-informed AI to reconstruct latent information embedded in complex multi-physical environments.

Rather than treating sensing as measurement, ARISE reframes it as:

inverse physical inference from resonant field dynamics.

ARISE SYSTEM

ARISE is not a single device or experiment.

It is a multi-layer physical information reconstruction architecture:

Layer 01 — Optical Resonator Core

High-Q whispering-gallery resonators enable ultra-sensitive encoding of external perturbations into stable optical eigenmodes.

Layer 02 — Photoacoustic Transduction Layer

Photoacoustic excitation introduces broadband physical perturbations that probe otherwise inaccessible material and fluid dynamics.

Layer 03 — AI Inference Engine

Physics-informed machine learning models reconstruct latent system states from coupled optical–acoustic signal manifolds.

Together, these layers form a unified framework for transforming physical interactions into interpretable information structures.

WHAT IS BEING ESTABLISHED

ARISE is not positioned as an incremental sensing improvement.

It defines a broader direction:

resonant-field-based physical information reconstruction.

This direction moves beyond conventional sensing, which relies on direct measurement or imaging, toward systems that infer hidden physical states from the dynamics of coupled fields.

In this sense, ARISE bridges:

  • optical resonator physics
  • photoacoustic signal generation
  • inverse problem theory
  • and data-driven physical inference

into a unified framework for complex environment sensing.

FUTURE DIRECTIONS

The long-term goal of ARISE is to establish a general class of resonant physical intelligence systems, capable of operating in environments where direct observation fails.

Key directions include:

  • Extending ARISE to heterogeneous and highly scattering media
  • Developing generalizable inverse models for multi-modal physical fields
  • Scaling from single-device sensing to distributed resonant sensing networks
  • Establishing a unified theory of resonant-field information encoding

Ultimately, ARISE aims to shift sensing from:

“detecting signals”

to

“reconstructing physical reality from resonant dynamics.”
