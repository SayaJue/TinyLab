Research Vision

Capturing whispers hidden in light, sound, and matter.

We design optical and optoacoustic micro-resonators, fuse physics-aware AI with hyperspectral sensing, and translate fundamental insights into sensitive, compact sensing systems for biological and environmental discovery.

Explore Research Selected Works

Research Vision

We pursue instrumentation and theory that reveal signals otherwise lost: from ultraweak photoacoustic traces to modal shifts in whispering-gallery resonators. Our goal is to build tools that let scientists "listen" to light and images with new sensitivity and interpretability.

Research Areas

Interdisciplinary work spanning experiment, theory, and computation.

Photoacoustic Sensing

High-sensitivity optical detection of acoustic responses for label-free imaging and sensing.

Whispering-Gallery-Mode Resonators

Design and control of microresonators for sensing, nonlinear optics, and quantum interfaces.

Physics-Informed AI

Hybrid models that merge first-principles constraints with data-driven learning for interpretable inference.

Hyperspectral Imaging

Multispectral and hyperspectral techniques for material, biomedical, and environmental characterization.

Approach

We iterate between device engineering, physical modeling, and constrained AI. Emphasis on compact devices, reproducible calibrations, and open data for community impact.

Representative Works

Multimode WGM barcode sensing

Multimode sensing by optical whispering-gallery-mode barcodes expands dynamic range for high-resolution measurement. (IEEE Transactions on Instrumentation and Measurement, 2024)

Biochemical sensing with WGM resonators

From whispering gallery mode resonators to biochemical sensors, demonstrating WGM-based detection for label-free material analysis. (ACS Sensors, 2023)

PDMS-backed displacement detection

High-Q WGM resonators encapsulated in PDMS for highly sensitive displacement detection in compact optical systems. (Journal of Lightwave Technology, 2023)
